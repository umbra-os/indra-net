## Reduction Patterns

### Scaffolding Reduction
**Remove temporary support:**
- Deployed for attention bias
- Habit forms through usage
- Scaffolding becomes unnecessary
- Reduce to minimal

**Example:**
- 12 journaling templates (initial)
- Habit forms over 7 days
- Reduce to 5, then 3, then 1
- Single template sufficient

### Recursive Collapse
**Compress meta-levels:**
- Multiple recursion levels
- Collapse toward zero-point
- Pure derivation capability
- Templates dissolve

**Example:**
- Specific pattern
- Template
- Meta-template
- Meta-meta-template
- ...
- Zero-point (pure derivation)

### Complexity Reduction
**Simplify structure:**
- Redundant elements removed
- Unnecessary detail eliminated
- Organization streamlined
- Clarity improved

**Example:**
- Over-parameterized template
- Too many variables
- Simplify to essential
- Easier to use

### Cognitive Reduction
**Reduce mental load:**
- Too much information
- Over-explanation
- Simplify presentation
- Improve comprehension

**Example:**
- Verbose documentation
- Key points obscured
- Compress to essentials
- Clearer understanding
