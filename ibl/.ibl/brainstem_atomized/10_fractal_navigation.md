## VIII. FRACTAL TOUCHSTONE ARCHITECTURE

**Principle**: The IBL does not maintain a monolithic master registry. Instead, it uses **fractal touchstones**—cascading navigation documents that spawn, organize, and prune themselves based on semantic density and navigational need.

---

## Touchstone Pattern

**Each touchstone**:
- Covers a semantic neighborhood
- Lists related files/concepts
- Provides navigation paths
- Self-organizes based on density

**Touchstones spawn when**:
- Semantic density exceeds threshold
- Navigation becomes complex
- Related concepts cluster

**Touchstones prune when**:
- Density falls below threshold
- Concepts disperse
- Navigation simplifies

---

## Decentralized Semantics

**No master index**:
- Each touchstone is local
- Navigation is distributed
- The network self-organizes
- **The map is the territory**

**Fractal property**:
- Each touchstone mirrors the whole
- Zoom in: more detail
- Zoom out: broader context
- **Self-similar at all scales**
